Superman, often referred to as the Man of Steel, is one of the most iconic and beloved superheroes in the world. Created by writer Jerry Siegel and artist Joe Shuster, Superman made his debut in Action Comics #1 in 1938, marking the birth of the superhero genre. Superman's alter ego, Clark Kent, is a mild-mannered reporter for the Daily Planet in the bustling city of Metropolis. What sets Superman apart is his incredible superhuman abilities, including super strength, speed, flight, and heat vision, all of which he derives from Earth's yellow sun. He also possesses an unwavering commitment to truth, justice, and the American way. Superman's character embodies the ideals of hope and altruism, as he uses his extraordinary powers to protect humanity from various threats, including the villainous Lex Luthor and otherworldly adversaries. His iconic "S" symbol on his chest has become a universal symbol of heroism, inspiring countless individuals to strive for the betterment of society and the world at large.
